An Ode to the Hamster (and an Example of Encoded Poetry)[1]
My tiny hamster, you inspire me to write.
I love the way you cuddle, squeak and skip,
Invading my mind day and through the night,
Always dreaming about the furry flip.
Let me compare you to a library?
You are more amazed, unfazed and superb.
Ice bites the debris of February,
And wintertime has the attractive blurb.
How do I love you? Let me count the ways.
I love your bold fingers, smile and eyebrows.
Thinking of your fuzzy smile fills my days.
My love for you is the inactive vows.
Now I must away with an active heart,
Remember my long words whilst we're apart.
