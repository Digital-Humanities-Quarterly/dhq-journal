Abstract

Welcome to the DHQ sample article. In this document, you should be able to find properly formatted examples of nearly every potential article element allowed for DHQ articles. For documentation on when or how to use these elements, consult the Encoding Documentation Wiki, available at: https://github.com/Digital-Humanities-Quarterly/dhq-journal/wiki/DHQ-Encoding-Documentation To see what most of these elements look like when passed through DHQ CSS, import the DHQ_Proof transformation scenario and open it in a web browser.

2: Poetry and Drama

2.1: Poetic Line Groups and Lines

3
An Ode to the Hamster (an example of encoded poetry)[1]
My tiny hamster, you inspire me to write.
I love the way you cuddle, squeak and skip,
Invading my mind day and through the night,
Always dreaming about the furry flip.
Let me compare you to a library?
You are more amazed, unfazed and superb.
Ice bites the debris of February,
And wintertime has the attractive blurb.
How do I love you? Let me count the ways.
I love your bold fingers, smile and eyebrows.
Thinking of your fuzzy smile fills my days.
My love for you is the inactive vows.
Now I must away with an active heart,
Remember my long words whilst we're apart.
