Search Help

The default search is a Boolean AND: the search will retrieve items containing all of the search terms. A Boolean OR search may be obtained by placing OR between the search terms (see example below). To search for a phrase, enclose the phrase within quotation marks; phrases may be used like individual words within a Boolean construction (see example below). Note that Boolean AND and OR operators must be capitalized.

A plus sign (+) preceding a search term indicates that this term is required; a minus sign (-) indicates that items including this term should be omitted from the search results. These operators may be combined within a single search as in the example below.

Sample Queries:
QuerySearch Result
book OR "digital humanities"Search for articles containing "book" or the phrase "digital humanities".
+digital humanitiesSearch for articles containing "digital" and preferentially containing "humanities".
electronic -digitalSearch for "electronic" but omit articles containing "digital".
affiliation:universitySearch for articles containing "university" in the affiliation element of the XML source.
bibl@id:smith*Search for articles containing the stem "smith" in the attribute of "id" within the element "bibl" of the XML source.
